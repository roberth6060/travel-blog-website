Denmark

Copenhagen, the capital of Denmark, is a vibrant and livable city with a population of over 1.3 million people in the metropolitan area (worldpopulationreview, 2023). Denmark is also known for being a social democracy, which means that it is a society that combines elements of capitalism with a strong welfare state. This system is characterized by a high level of social and economic equality, as well as a commitment to universal healthcare, education, and social services. The city has a reputation for being one of the happiest cities in the world, and living there for a year its not hard to see why. From my experience the people there are always happy and always enjoy spending time off work with family and friend, which the locals decribe as "hygge."

Japan

Living in Tokyo, Japan for a few months was an incredible and unforgettable experience. As one of the world's largest and most vibrant cities, Tokyo offers a unique blend of ancient traditions and cutting-edge modernity, making it a fascinating place to explore and discover especially for individual that are avid anime watcher and manga reader that is why I was elated when I was offered the opportunity to work temporarily there in 2016 at U.S. Embassy Japan. One of the first things I noticed upon arriving Minato-Ku, Tokyo is how safe and clean the city is. Crime rates are very low, and the streets are meticulously maintained, making it a pleasant and easy place to navigate. Slovenia

The trip to Ljubljana, Slovenia was a spontaneous trip with friends during the summer in 2018. We were bored and decided to travel to Slovenia due to its close proximity to Zagreb, Croatia. So, we drove over 1 hour from Croatia to Slovenia to explore the charming streets and experience the unique culture of this incredible destination. When we arrived in Slovenia we primarily walked through the city center, and was astonished by the colorful architecture and lively atmosphere of the city. The pedestrian-only streets made it easy to explore the city as everything we needed was within a walking distance. The city was so easy to navigate we walked for hours until we come across the magnificent Ljubljanski grad, the ancient castle that sits atop a hill overlooking the city. My friends and I decided to make the climb up to the castle, eager to explore the fascinating history and take in the breathtaking views from the top.
